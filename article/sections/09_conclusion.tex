\section{Conclusion}

This article presented a simulation-based study of low-height distributed-\gls{mimo} access networks with repositionable infrastructure in an outdoor urban scenario. The paper combined a system model, three relocation algorithms, a reproducible Rabot simulation workflow, and postprocessed geometry, \gls{sinr}, \gls{esr}, and relocation figures derived from exported artifacts.

Within its current scope, the framework supports systematic comparison of a central rooftop proxy, a fixed distributed deployment, and three relocation-capable distributed deployments under a common channel model and mobility process. Natural next steps are multi-scenario studies, coverage-map analysis, and experimental validation in the broader Cocoon program.

Figure~\ref{fig:sinr_primary_pair} establishes a clear ordering. The nearest-user proxy-\gls{esr} controller of optimization~2 dominates almost the full empirical \gls{sinr} range. Its mean \gls{sinr} reaches \SI{39.7}{dB}, with a 10th percentile of \SI{24.5}{dB}; the fixed distributed baseline reaches \SI{21.3}{dB} mean and \SI{7.5}{dB} at the 10th percentile. The outage fraction below \SI{0}{dB} drops from \SI{4.41}{\percent} for the fixed baseline to \SI{1.87}{\percent} for optimization~2. Optimization~3 is the second-best radio strategy with \SI{35.7}{dB} mean \gls{sinr} and \SI{16.3}{dB} 10th-percentile \gls{sinr}, but its outage fraction remains essentially identical to the fixed baseline at about \SI{4.42}{\percent}. Hence, the power-only proxy improves the middle and upper portions of the distribution much more strongly than the weakest-link tail.

The central rooftop proxy is weakest throughout, with \SI{9.8}{dB} mean \gls{sinr}, \SI{-22.1}{dB} 10th-percentile \gls{sinr}, and \SI{18.4}{\percent} outage. The per-user mean boxplot reinforces the same message: optimization~2 lifts all users into a narrow \SIrange{36.4}{42.5}{dB} band, optimization~3 places all users in a lower but still clearly elevated \SIrange{31.1}{38.8}{dB} band, whereas the central proxy exhibits the widest user imbalance and even negative per-user means for the weakest users.

The \gls{esr} figures in \cref{fig:esr_primary_pair} follow the same ranking. Mean \gls{esr} reaches \SI{133.1}{bit/s/Hz} for optimization~2, \SI{122.8}{bit/s/Hz} for optimization~3, \SI{74.9}{bit/s/Hz} for the fixed distributed baseline, and \SI{56.4}{bit/s/Hz} for the central rooftop proxy. Relative to the fixed baseline, optimization~2 improves the mean \gls{esr} by \SI{77.8}{\percent} and the 10th-percentile \gls{esr} by \SI{88.8}{\percent}. Optimization~3 still improves the mean \gls{esr} by \SI{64.1}{\percent}, but remains consistently below optimization~2. Window by window, optimization~2 attains the highest mean \gls{esr} in 42 of the 61 relocation windows and exceeds the fixed baseline in all windows; optimization~3 leads in 15 windows and exceeds the fixed baseline in 57 windows. The relocation markers every \SI{60}{\second} make the controller action visible as a sequence of step changes, and the highest sustained post-relocation levels occur for optimization~2.

Together, these figures show that the best result in this scenario is not merely ``more movement'' or ``more power'', but the combination of relocation with a phase-aware \gls{ue}-to-\gls{ue} proxy-\gls{csi} objective. The fixed distributed baseline remains a strong zero-relocation benchmark, whereas the central rooftop proxy is clearly mismatched to the spatially spread Rabot user process.

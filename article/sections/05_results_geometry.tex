\Cref{fig:geometry_pair} provides the geometric context for the subsequent \gls{sinr}, \gls{esr}, and relocation analyses. The scene-layout panel depicts the candidate-site pool and the selected constellation, whereas the trajectory overlay shows the spatial spread of the simulated user population.
For the present Rabot instance, the candidate-generation procedure yields \num{87} candidate low-height mounting locations, from which \num{6} distributed sites are activated per distributed strategy. The simulated mobility trace contributes \num{7210} user-snapshot positions over the \SI{1}{\hour} horizon, and these positions span most of the accessible outdoor footprint rather than a single narrow corridor. This geometry anticipates a harder coverage problem for the single rooftop proxy than for wall-mounted distributed deployments, because the user population repeatedly moves through streets and courtyard edges that can be served from multiple nearby façades.

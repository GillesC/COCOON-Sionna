\section{Discussion and Current Limits}

The present study has several modeling and evaluation limits that define the scope of the reported results.

Three findings stand out from the Rabot results. First, distributed wall-mounted deployments are consistently superior to the normalized central rooftop proxy in this scenario. Even the static distributed baseline improves both average and lower-tail performance over \texttt{central\_massive\_mimo}, which indicates that the spatially spread outdoor user process benefits strongly from nearby façade-mounted service points. Second, the quality of the relocation proxy matters more than relocation alone. Optimization~2 outperforms optimization~3 on all reported radio metrics while also requiring less movement, which shows that phase-aware \gls{ue}-to-\gls{ue} proxy \gls{csi} carries useful information beyond power-only proxy measurements. Third, the optimization objective matters as much as the proxy source. Optimization~1 often improves window-average \gls{esr}, but it still produces a markedly worse lower tail than the fixed baseline. In the present dataset, local historical best-user \gls{sinr} optimization is therefore not sufficient to control trajectory-level outage robustness.

The main limitations are the following:
\begin{itemize}
    \item Only a single outdoor scenario is considered.
    \item The \texttt{central\_massive\_mimo} comparison point is a rooftop proxy, not a full placement model for one true co-located massive-\gls{mimo} array.
    \item The current Rabot configuration disables coverage-map exports, so the paper centers on trajectory-level \gls{sinr} and schedule metrics.
    \item Citizen participation is abstracted as movable candidate positions and scheduler actions rather than being validated experimentally.
    \item Sensitivity to random seeds, scenario perturbations, and broader deployment-cost models is not yet characterized.
\end{itemize}

These limitations motivate extensions toward multi-scenario studies, sensitivity analyses, coverage-map reporting, and eventual experimental validation in co-created deployments.

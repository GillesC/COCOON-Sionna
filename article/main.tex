\begin{abstract}
This paper studies low-height distributed-\gls{mimo} access networks with repositionable access points in an outdoor urban setting. Using the \texttt{COCOON-Sionna} framework, the workflow generates wall-mounted candidate \glspl{ap}, simulates pedestrian and bicycle mobility, computes path-based \gls{csi}, and compares a central rooftop proxy, a fixed distributed deployment, and three relocation-capable distributed deployments over a common scenario. The article formalizes the \gls{sinr}- and \gls{esr}-based evaluation model, a history-aware $K$-nearest relocation heuristic, a distance-threshold sum-rate relocation heuristic, a distance-threshold average-power relocation heuristic, and the postprocessing workflow that produces geometry, performance, and relocation-cost figures from reproducible simulation outputs.
\end{abstract}
